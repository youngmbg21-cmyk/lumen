A recommendation engine, not a catalogue

Taste-aware book discovery for adult readers.

Lumen takes your reading preferences, boundaries, and stylistic sensibilities, then scores a library of works to surface titles that genuinely fit — with a transparent explanation for every match and confidence score.

What it does

Captures structured reader preferences, applies weighted fit scoring, filters hard exclusions, and ranks titles with explainable reasons for each match.

How to use

  1. Set your preferences in the Reader Profile.
  2. Review weights and filters in the Fit Engine.
  3. Explore ranked recommendations with explanations.
  4. Try scenarios or compare titles side-by-side.

Engine logic & transparency

The full rulebook for how matches are scored. Nothing is hidden — every number in a recommendation is traceable to one of these rules.

1 — Input normalisation

Your profile is normalised into a canonical preference vector covering numeric dimensions (heat, explicitness, emotional intensity, consent clarity, taboo tolerance, plot-vs-scene) and categorical sets (tones, pacing, dynamics, tropes, kinks, orientation, literary style).

profile = normalizeProfile(rawInputs)

2 — Hard exclusions

Any book tagged with content you've added to hard exclusions is removed from ranking entirely. It never scores, never appears — it's gone from the recommendation pool.

if intersect(book.exclusionTags, profile.hardNo).length > 0 → drop

3 — Weighted fit score

For each surviving book, the engine evaluates each attribute against your preference and applies the corresponding weight. Scores are graded, not binary:

  • Exact match on numeric dimension → full credit × weight
  • Near match (off by 1 step) → 60% credit × weight
  • Partial match (off by 2) → 25% credit × weight
  • Tag overlap — credit scales with proportion of your selected tags present
  • Disliked tag present — penalty applied × weight
baseScore = Σ (attrMatch(book, profile) × weight[attr])
finalScore = baseScore − warningPenalty − dislikePenalty

4 — Confidence score

Confidence reflects how many of your preference signals the book has rich metadata for. A book with sparse tagging gets downgraded because the match — even if high — is built on fewer signals.

confidence = (signalsMatched / totalPreferenceSignals) × metadataDensity

5 — Warning-aware adjustment

Content warnings are surfaced regardless of rank. Your warning strictness setting controls how much they shift the score:

  • Lenient — warnings shown but no score penalty
  • Moderate — small penalty per warning (default)
  • Strict — meaningful penalty per warning, and a book with 4+ warnings is automatically deprioritised

6 — Why this matched

The explanation strings you see on each recommendation are generated from the actual attributes that contributed most to that book's score. If a book's ranked #1 because you prioritised literary style and it aligned, the explanation will say so — not generic marketing copy.

explanation = generateWhyItMatched(topContributors, profile, book)

7 — Scenario overlay

Scenarios aren't filters — they're full preference profiles that replace your current profile. Switching back restores your saved profile. Re-ranking is immediate because scoring runs in-browser.

Engineering mapping

For readers curious about the architecture — these are the core functions, each a direct remap from the VCFO engine philosophy:

  • normalizeProfile() — inputs / profile capture
  • evaluateBookFit() — diagnostics / per-book analysis
  • scoreBook() — weighted scoring
  • applyHardExclusions() — risk filters / hard-no rules
  • rankRecommendations() — prioritised output
  • generateWhyItMatched() — explainability
  • compareBooks() — side-by-side evaluation
  • runScenario() — what-if modelling
